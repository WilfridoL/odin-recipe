Potato stuffed

Stuffed potatoes are very popular in Colombia. We eat them for breakfast or as a snack. Papas Rellenas can be served hot or cold and are delicious with chili.

Colombians have different versions of Papas Rellenas and each family has their own recipe, I called my aunt in Florida to get my grandmother's recipe to make these potatoes. Some people add boiled egg and rice to the filling but we don't do it.

INGREDIENTS

  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 5 medium potatoes
  • ½ teaspoon salt

Meat stuffing

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped tomato
  • ⅓ cup chopped onion
  • ⅓ cup chopped long onion
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon Sazón with saffron
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon ground pepper
  • ½ pound ground beef

Colada

  • 1 egg
  • ¼ cup flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • ½ tablespoon Sazón with saffron
  • ¼ cup milk

STEPS

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into chunks, put them in a medium saucepan and cover with water and add ½ teaspoon of salt.
  2. Bring the potatoes to a boil over medium-high heat, then lower the heat to medium and cook until the potatoes are soft about 20 minutes. Drain the potatoes and mash them as for mashing with a fork or masher and set aside.
  3. Drain the potatoes and mash them as for mashing with a fork or masher and set aside.
  4. Prepare the stuffing, in a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat and add the onion, tomato, large onion, garlic, cumin, saffron, pepper and salt. Cook for 5 minutes or until the onion is transparent, add the meat and cook stirring occasionally for 10-15 minutes. Let cool.
  5. To make the colada: In a bowl mix all the ingredients and whisk until the mixture is smooth and set aside.
  6. Divide the mashed potatoes into 10 equal sized portions, about 1/ 3 cup each, shape into a ball and with your fingers make a hole and place 1 ½ tablespoons of filling in the hole.
  7. Cover the hole with a little more puree, to completely enclose the filling. Repeat the procedure with the rest of the dumplings.
  8. Fill a large, heavy pot with vegetable oil and heat over medium-high heat to 360°F.
  9. Dip the stuffed potatoes in the batter and carefully place them in the hot oil and fry for about 4 minutes or until golden brown. Remove the potatoes from the oil and drain on a plate with paper towels. Transfer to a plate and serve with Ají.
